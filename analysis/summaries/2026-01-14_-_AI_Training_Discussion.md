# AI Training Discussion
**Date:** 2026-01-14
**Attendees:** Colin Holmes, Pietro (learning specialist), Stephanie (training KPIs), Richard Dosoo, Azmain Hossain
**Duration context:** Medium (~28 minutes)
**Workstreams touched:** WS1 (Training & Enablement)

## Key Points
- Colin Holmes explains the insurance AI steerco, established mid-2025 at Diya/Mike's request. Purpose: inventory AI activities across insurance, prioritise for 2026, establish an evaluation framework, and improve cross-functional collaboration. It was always meant to be insurance-specific, not cross-OU.
- Colin signals the steerco has largely done its job (inventorying and prioritising) and the next step is building a broader operating model for AI within insurance, with formal linkages to banking and other OUs.
- Pietro gives critical advice: do not start with training. Start by identifying the AI capabilities each project requires, then assess team proficiency against those capabilities, then design learning. Training should come last, not first.
- Stephanie emphasises planning with the end in mind: define what success looks like (KPIs), then work backwards. Asks pointed questions about measurement -- is success 95% of people meeting their AI objectives? Usage dashboards? Customer feedback on speed?
- Azmain proposes a solution-focused training approach: bucket training by outcome (dashboarding, presentations, etc.) rather than by tool. This way, people self-select based on what they want to accomplish, and each training teaches both the AI tool skills and the domain skills (e.g., how to present data effectively, not just how to use Cursor).
- Richard notes this flips the previous training approach from tool-focused to solution-focused, which will require more domain-specific content and use cases.
- Stephanie advocates for a train-the-trainer model: identify SMEs within the business who can deliver training at scale, reducing dependency on Pietro and Stephanie.
- Stephanie has already refreshed some of the previous CS training content and it is on Moody's University as a playlist.
- Colin wants to understand nuances across insurance sub-segments (casualty vs PMS vs life) to tailor training appropriately.

## Decisions Made
- Training will be reorganised from tool-focused to solution-focused buckets (dashboarding, presentations, analysis, etc.) -> Azmain/Richard to define buckets
- Will conduct proficiency assessment survey before designing new training -> Pietro/Stephanie
- Train-the-trainer approach adopted for scale -> Stephanie to identify SMEs over time
- Training becomes the sixth formal project/workstream in the programme -> Azmain
