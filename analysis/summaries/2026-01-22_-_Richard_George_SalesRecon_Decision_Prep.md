## Narrative Notes
This conversation crystallized the central strategic tension of Week 3: the team is building multiple tools (CLARA, account planner, sentiment dashboard) at remarkable speed, but the sustainability question looms. Richard wants Sales Recon to absorb everything so the team is not stuck maintaining products. George wants flexibility and speed. Azmain sees consolidation as the answer. The truth is probably that all three perspectives contain wisdom: some capabilities should converge into Sales Recon (long-term), some should consolidate into CLARA (medium-term), and some need to remain flexible for rapid iteration (short-term). The manual-first approach George proposed for account planning is the kind of change management wisdom that often gets lost in the excitement of AI-powered development — it is easier to digitize a process that people already follow than to impose a digital process on people who have never done the underlying work. Diya's insistence on outcome-based milestones rather than another alignment conversation adds urgency to Monday's meeting: the executives want to see progress, not plans.
