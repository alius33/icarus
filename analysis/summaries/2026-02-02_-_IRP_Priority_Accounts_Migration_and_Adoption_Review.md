## Narrative Notes
This meeting is a watershed moment for the programme. The first live use of CLARA in a real Portfolio Review represents months of work by Azmain, Ben, and BenVH coming to fruition. That it was immediately overshadowed by a data loss incident is painful but instructive -- it reveals the gap between feature development velocity and deployment reliability. Natalia's handling of the crisis is exemplary: she acknowledged the problem, allowed George to assess the damage, and steered the meeting back to its purpose. The fact that CSMs were engaged enough to have entered data over the weekend is, paradoxically, a positive signal about adoption. Richard's later reflection that this was the first structured, data-driven portfolio review in the organisation's history puts the significance in perspective. The data loss did not kill momentum, but it created a trust debt that will take weeks to repay.
