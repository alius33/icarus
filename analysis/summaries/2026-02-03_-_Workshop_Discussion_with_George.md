## Key Points
- George planning a two-day CSM workshop — CLARA is now a key component of the agenda
- Workshop structure: background sync discussion, then hands-on sprints where CSMs get into the records, update data, find issues, and provide feedback
- George wants Azmain in the room so feedback can be captured and potentially some fixes made semi-live
- Key framing from George: this should NOT feel like another introduction — most people in the room should have already used CLARA. The tone should be enabling immediate activity, not getting started.
- George clearly distinguished CLARA's scope (IRP adoption/migration) from Gainsight's scope (overall customer health) — they overlap but are not the same
- Example: a customer can have green overall health but a red IRP migration project, or vice versa (e.g., Zurich Insurance — good IRP progress but commercially risky)
- George wants a co-demo with a CSM who has actively used the tool — not just Azmain presenting to a passive audience
- Azmain mentioned the Gainsight alignment strategy: position CLARA as a stopgap that feeds into Gainsight, not a replacement
- George suggested the workshops cover account planning on the second day — which aligns with broader Salesforce/Gainsight goals
- George asked Julia to coordinate with Azmain on the introduction and find a CSM to co-present

## Decisions Made
- Workshop should be enabling and action-oriented, not introductory → George
- CLARA scope is explicitly IRP adoption/migration; Gainsight handles overall customer health → George/Azmain agreed
- A CSM should co-demo with Azmain to show ownership → George
