# Meeting with Divya on AI Programme Governance
**Date:** 2026-02-11
**Attendees:** Divya (Speaker 1), Idrees, Richard Dosoo
**Duration context:** Short-medium (~17 minutes)
**Workstreams touched:** WS2 CLARA, WS3 CS Agent (Sales Recon), WS4 Friday (Adoption Charter — governance layer)

## Key Points
- This is a major alignment session. Richard and Idrees are briefing Divya on the full context of what the insurance CS team has been doing with AI tooling.
- Idrees provides the essential backstory for Divya: the RMS acquisition created a gap where insurance CS does not have Gainsight working. Charlotte's team is launching MA 3.0 in March for existing OUs, but RMS data is not included. Because of this gap, Richard's team proactively built their own tool (CLARA) using Cursor — described by Idrees as an impressive initiative that should not be downplayed.
- The Sales Recon pilot launched today with 8 CSMs. The pilot will feed requirements into Anna's team (on Jamie's roadmap) who are building CS-specific features. Jamie is supportive but his team is small and does not have a mandate to build a tool specifically for the insurance group.
- Divya asks three key questions: (1) What are the efficiency metrics/KPIs? She wants to measure ROI and prove the hypothesis that AI is helping. (2) Is Sales Recon the biggest bang for the buck, or are there other AI solutions to consider? She mentions customer onboarding as an example — possibly using tools like Anthropic's or OpenAI's agent kit for low-touch/tier-3 self-serve. (3) She wants the next meeting (next week) to be "more philosophical" about priorities rather than diving into specifics.
- Divya raises the importance of not duplicating efforts. She asks whether Dominic's team (doing retro churn analysis) is aligned with the forward-looking work Richard and Idrees are doing. Idrees explains: Dominic's team looks backward to identify churn drivers; their team looks forward to prevent churn. Cyril from Dominic's team is included in the Sales Recon pilot to stay aligned.
- Richard offers to provide "directional KPIs" about the projects they are working on to help drive next week's discussion.
- Divya frames the philosophical question: the company is going through headcount reductions that impact service quality. How can AI bridge that gap? That is the lens she wants applied to all AI investments.
- Idrees claims he can demonstrate 15-30 hours per week savings per CSM through the initiatives being measured via Charlotte's Gainsight launch in March. He has two CSMs in the Sales Recon pilot who will provide before/after comparison data.

- Divya's mention of headcount reductions as the backdrop for AI investment is politically significant — she is framing AI as a response to resource constraints, which could strengthen the business case but also creates pressure to deliver quantifiable savings.
