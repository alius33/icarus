# CSM Workshop Session 1 — George's Team in London
**Date:** 2026-02-12
**Attendees:** George Dyke (Speaker 1), Ronald (Speaker 2), Speaker 3 (likely European-based CSM), Miles (Speaker 5), Ashley (Speaker 6), Philip (Speaker 4), Speaker 7 (Rachel Gillespie), Azmain Hossain (Speaker 8), Natalia, Steve Gentilly (Speaker 9), Liz Couchman (Speaker 8 in later section), Thomas Harrison (Speaker 9)
**Duration context:** Long (multi-hour workshop, transcript covers ~50 minutes of substantive discussion)
**Workstreams touched:** WS1 Training, WS2 CLARA, WS4 Friday (Adoption Charter)

## Key Points
- This is Day 1 of a two-day in-person CSM workshop in London organised by George. The group reflects on Day 1 learnings before pivoting to IRP tooling and adoption squads.
- Communication gaps identified: CSMs were unaware that the sales team has restructured — property workflow specialists are now "relationship managers," there is a casualty relationship manager role, and cyber may move to the casualty side. This information only surfaced during a call with Ursula and Jennifer. The sales team does not send broad communications about structural changes.
- Ronald raises the need for better collaboration with the product team (not just sales). Model product managers have goals for new licences by HD model — CSMs were not aware of this. George agrees this is a gap and plans follow-up conversations with Lonnie, Maurizio, and Julie.
- Score adoption is presented as a case study by Miles. The adoption squad model is working: Katya (sales), Miles (CSM), Liz Couchman (solutions architect), Kristin Hilton (PM), Cherish Cricket (functional implementation lead), Emily Walton (technical), and Pratique Frank (analytical services). Kristin as PM has been instrumental — setting up regular cadence with Score's PM, managing communications, creating SharePoint for shared docs, logging questions.
- Score initially rejected the adoption charter (took 6 weeks to respond, then politely declined — they had their own plan). Lesson learned: having a solutions architect present at the initial charter conversation would have been better. The team pivoted to aligning their support with Score's own timeline rather than imposing the charter.
- Score asked for 600 vulnerability curves when offered 5-6. The aligned adoption squad was able to push back consistently with one voice because everyone was on the same page.
- Rachel Gillespie explains the implementation lead role: for non-consulting-bank clients, they offer two workshops plus two follow-up sessions. They are deliberately limiting free support to push clients toward implementation partners. This is a cultural shift from the historical "yes, we'll do that for you" approach.
- Thomas Harrison (model specialist lead) explains the specialist team focus for the year: getting clients onto HD models, RQD pipeline, financial model challenges especially on reinsurance side.
- Introductions from Natalia (enablement and execution team, programme governance), Steve Gentilly (solution blueprints, solution fit analysis), and Liz Couchman (solutions architect, 7 years of IRP implementation experience).
