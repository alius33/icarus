## Key Points
- Meeting starts with extended political discussion (Prince Andrew, US politics, Bill Gates) before pivoting to work at ~5:44.
- Liz provides substantive feedback on adoption charters and how they should integrate with CLARA. Two key concepts emerge: (1) Strategic objectives -- the big-picture "why did they license IRP" that should sit prominently on each customer's front page in CLARA, and (2) Target objectives (per phase/use case) -- what CSMs currently call "success criteria" but should be renamed for clarity.
- Liz confirms adoption charter templates are fully defined, in use (though not widely enough), and have been established since October/November. There are two types: in-flight and pipeline. The 17-18 "accelerated" accounts have been identified as needing adoption charters.
- Azmain admits he did not know the adoption charter structure was already defined. This is a significant information gap.
- The group agrees on quick wins: (1) Add a "strategic objectives" box to the customer front page in CLARA, (2) Rename "success criteria" to "target objectives" for naming consistency with charters, (3) Add an adoption charter status field (not started / in progress / agreed) visible in portfolio reviews.
- Liz offers to be added to the team chat to provide pushback on people who resist moving beyond the golden source mentality.
- Azmain raises the cultural challenge: some stakeholders (Catherine, Josh) are emotionally attached to the golden source Excel format and resist process changes. Liz agrees to help provide a counterweight.
- Azmain notes he is pausing feature development until after the Diya governance meeting on 25 Feb -- priority shifts to preparing the presentation.
