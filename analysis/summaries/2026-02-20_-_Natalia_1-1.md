## Narrative Notes
This meeting is the most important governance conversation of Week 7. For the first time, Azmain formally escalates the resource crisis to his manager and they agree on a structured path to executive sign-off. The conversation reveals the severity of the situation: five of six workstreams stalled, one person doing everything, and the leadership is about to start asking questions that the team cannot answer without a plan.

Natalia's response is exactly right. She does not panic, she does not dismiss. She immediately asks: who decides? what resources? what timeline? And she commits to having the difficult resourcing conversation with Ben Brooks. This is a manager who understands that her report is drowning and is prepared to fight for structural relief rather than just expressing sympathy.

The project management feature that Azmain demos is both inspiring and concerning. It is inspiring because it shows his capacity to identify needs (Diana's team runs project calls from PowerPoint and Excel) and build solutions quickly. It is concerning because it is on his personal laptop, not in version control, not prioritised by anyone, and represents yet another demand on his already impossible bandwidth. This is the third parallel build track (CLARA + PM feature + eventual programme work) that Azmain is carrying, and it is unsustainable.

The feature freeze decision is wise. The team has been in a cycle of constant releases and constant feedback, creating a treadmill effect where Azmain can never step back to think strategically. The pause until after the Diya meeting creates breathing room -- but only if the Diya meeting actually produces results (resource allocation, prioritisation clarity). If Diya does not engage, the pause just delays the treadmill.

Natalia's framing -- "it's almost like a startup... everyone does everything, and it's very agile, but now we have the growing pains" -- is the most accurate description of the programme's current state. The question is whether the organisation can transition from startup mode to sustained operations without losing the speed that made CLARA possible in the first place.
