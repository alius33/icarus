## Key Points
- The long-awaited governance session with Diya -- the single most important governance moment since the programme began. Richard presents three programme pillars: (1) Governance of IRP portfolio (CLARA + George's account planner), (2) Platform embedded intelligence (Sales Recon data pipeline, IRP Navigator L1 automation, Courtney's support analysis), (3) Internal productivity and revenue acceleration (App Factory, Build in Five, content platform).
- Natalia contributes a strategically important insight: CLARA is not IRP-specific and could be reused for other programmes (EGL, Hosted), but advises building for a specific programme first before generalising. She has already discussed this with Julien from Life.
- Ben reveals plans to demo CLARA architecture to Christos's (Life) leadership team -- a cross-OU evangelism play to create independent demand for investment.
- Diya appears engaged for the first time substantively. She directs the team not to add Life to CLARA's roadmap -- keep it focused on insurance IRP. Sales Recon is the only area requiring joined-up roadmaps across OUs.
- Diya raises the critical post-12-week question: what are the outcomes? Efficiency vs productivity. Ben counters that CSMs could manage 10 accounts instead of 5 with the right tools.
- Resource plan discussed: 12-week runway with Martin (75%), Chris (50%), Nikhil (50%), BenVH (50%), rotating grad from April 7. Beyond 12 weeks, the team needs a business case for a dedicated team of 3-5 people.
- Maps team dynamics shift dramatically: now that the programme has demonstrated value, the Maps team is offering to help -- previously they refused, saying it was internal-only and "not important enough."
- Azmain makes the bus factor explicit for the first time in front of leadership.
