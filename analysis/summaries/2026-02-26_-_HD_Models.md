# HD Models
**Date:** 2026-02-26
**Attendees:** Richard Dosoo, Azmain Hossain, Courtney Spillers (Speaker 1), Carlin, Lucia, Bala (Speaker 2)
**Duration context:** Short (~15 minutes, partial transcript)
**Workstreams touched:** WS2 CLARA, WS3 CS Agent

## Key Points
- Ben (Brooks) connected Richard and Azmain with Courtney Spillers' team because Courtney has been working on HD model adoption intelligence using Salesforce support case data -- significant crossover with the programme's platform embedded intelligence pillar.
- Courtney's team (with Ben Reynolds and Matt Allen) used Cursor to analyse 2,000-3,000 HD-related support cases. They identified two buckets of adoption barriers: (1) enablement gaps -- customers do not understand how to use HD models, either because they are not referencing available materials or because guidance does not exist for their specific business workflow use case (DLM-to-HD transition caveats), and (2) product gaps -- missing functionality that prevents embedding HD into business workflows.
- The critical distinction: HD adoption is not about viewing risk (customers can do that), it is about embedding HD into business workflows. The transition from DLM to HD introduces caveats around grouping, roll-up, PT basis, and business type that are not well documented.
- Courtney is being repeatedly asked whether this intelligence can be surfaced in CLARA or Ben's dashboard. She clarifies her primary goal is an operations dashboard (hence Carlin and Lucia attending), but acknowledges the aggregate HD blocker data should flow into the overall IRP adoption storyboard.
- The aggregate vs. per-customer distinction matters: aggregate analysis helps product teams prioritise roadmap and capacity. Per-customer analysis helps CSMs. The support case data is better suited for aggregate themes than individual customer tracking, since not every customer raises tickets for every issue.
- Richard explains the gap between CLARA's blockers (CSM-entered, focused on switch-off status) and Salesforce case data (more granular HD adoption issues). Using Sompo as an example: CLARA shows adoption underway with a few blockers, but Salesforce reveals a much more detailed story about HD-specific challenges.
- Frequency of analysis: Courtney suggests monthly rather than daily or weekly, since the adoption blocker footprint does not change rapidly. Bala suggests it might need to be faster after product releases (e.g., Moody's SSO migration) to capture feedback quickly.
- Richard offers two paths: (1) surface the data in CLARA (can be added to the feature backlog), or (2) teach Courtney's team to update CLARA code directly, since it is straightforward. The data pipeline the programme is building for Salesforce/Mixpanel would also serve Courtney's needs.
- The Sales Recon dependency: the programme had planned to use Sales Recon as the data pipeline, but Bernard's UAT revealed it is not ready. An interim self-built pipeline is now needed, which can serve both the core programme and Courtney's operations dashboard requirements.

## Decisions Made
- HD blocker intelligence to be added to CLARA backlog for future integration -> Richard / Azmain
- Aggregate HD analysis to be refreshed monthly (not daily or weekly) -> Courtney
- Data pipeline being built for Salesforce/Mixpanel to serve both programme and Courtney's team -> Richard
