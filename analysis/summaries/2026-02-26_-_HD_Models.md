# HD Models — Courtney Spillers Requirements Session
**Date:** 2026-02-26

**Duration context:** Short (~15 minutes, partial transcript)
**Workstreams touched:** WS2 CLARA, WS3 CS Agent

## Key Points
- Courtney's team (Ben Reynolds, Matt Allen) analysed 2,000-3,000 support cases related to HD models using Cursor. They found two buckets of adoption barriers: (1) enablement gaps (customers do not understand how to use HD models, DLM-to-HD transition caveats), and (2) product gaps (missing functionality that blocks business workflow integration).
- Critical distinction: HD model adoption is not about viewing risk -- it is about embedding HD into business workflows. The caveats around DLM vs HD grouping, roll-up, PT basis, and multi-analysis scenarios are where customers get stuck.
- Courtney does not want this data primarily in CLARA. Her goal is an operations dashboard for her team (hence Carlin and Lucia attending). But she is being asked by leadership to surface HD adoption intelligence in the broader IRP adoption story.
- The data currently exists as a one-time analysis. Refresh frequency debated: monthly is the consensus, not weekly (the footprint does not change fast enough), but Bala notes SSO migration type events might need faster feedback.
- Richard proposes feeding the data into CLARA's blocker page. Courtney notes CLARA's blocker data (CSM-entered) tells a different story than the Salesforce case data (customer-entered). Example: Sompo appears to be progressing in CLARA but has much more granular HD adoption issues in Salesforce.
- The aggregate view is more important than per-customer right now -- product team needs to understand the overall adoption barrier themes to prioritise their roadmap.
- Courtney's team needs a data pipeline regardless. Richard proposes combining this with the interim Salesforce/Mixpanel pipeline they are already building (since Sales Recon failed UAT).
- Richard offers to show Courtney's team how to update CLARA code themselves -- collaborative development model.
