# How Credit Team in Banking Does AI Training
**Date:** 2026-03-13
**Attendees:** Richard Dosoo, Nils (Credit team Banking, research lead), Wasim (Credit team Banking, co-lead), Georgie (Credit team Banking, attendee), Bridget (insurance team)
**Duration context:** Medium (~45 minutes)
**Primary project:** Cross OU Collaboration
**Secondary projects:** Training & Enablement

## Key Points
- Banking Credit team ran structured "Agent Days" — dedicated full-day in-person events for AI adoption across their team
- Four-tier agent model introduced: (1) Copilot Light, (2) Copilot Studio, (3) Cursor coding agents with skills/MCPs/sub-agents, (4) Custom-built agents from scratch. Most participants targeted tier 3.
- Critical success factors for agent days: **pre-work is everything** — surveys sent weeks before to identify use cases and experience levels, all technical setup (Cursor install, MCP connections, skills) completed before the day, and "transit teachers" (4+ trained multipliers) pre-briefed on each participant's project
- Every participant was required to build their own agent by end of day — not group work, individual projects. Every person succeeded, including non-coders.
- Non-coder highlight: a banking strategy team member who normally spends a week on market sizing had it done with slides in Moody's format by 4pm on agent day — from zero to working agent in one afternoon session
- Agent day structure: 1-hour intro (MCP live demo, skills/rules/sub-agents concepts), then alternating 60-90 minute build sessions with 20-30 minute regroup/present sessions (3-4 touchpoints per day)
- Deliberate choice to have non-experts present their work rather than power users — this motivated others more effectively
- Code validation approach: independent sub-agents checking other agents' output in separate contexts. Nils describes this as the evolution from "read every line manually" to "use agents to validate agents"
- Skills, rules, and sub-agents shared via GitHub for cross-team reuse. Team maintains a shared GitHub folder of curated rules and skills.
- Bi-weekly knowledge-sharing sessions maintain momentum post-agent day — not a one-off event
- Richard acknowledged the insurance team's November 2025 training was too generic and suffered from the same installation-day problems Banking solved with pre-work
- Richard proposed homework on insurance side: build internal skills repo, establish governance model, identify coordinator
- Agreed next steps: cross-OU collaboration via quarterly meetups, shared materials, and a coordinator model (not ad-hoc support)
