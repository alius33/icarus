## Executive Summary

The Customer Success Gen AI Programme formally launched this week with Richard Dosoo briefing Azmain Hossain as programme manager across all six workstreams. What was previously a collection of ad-hoc projects crystallised into a connected programme with a coherent narrative: from adoption charter through CLARA tracking to reporting, with Sales Recon as the long-term strategic home. Richard framed this explicitly as a career vehicle for Azmain, tying programme success to his June promotion case.

The week's defining moment came on January 6 when Ben Brooks revealed the CLARA v1 prototype he built solo over Christmas using Cursor. It far exceeded the original dashboard scope, introducing enforced process rigour (every non-green use case must have a blocker, every blocker must have an action plan) and exposing a devastating data quality finding: updating the golden source moved completed migrations from 28 to 41 -- twelve completions were invisible because nobody had updated the data. This single finding validated the entire CLARA thesis while revealing the cultural challenge ahead.

Technically, the week was consumed by deployment warfare. Azure hosting was blocked by corporate security constraints, leading to a decisive AWS pivot on January 7 after IT experts acknowledged they could not help and redirected to yet more people. BenVH emerged as the deployment hero, offering to stand up AWS infrastructure using personal CI/CD scripts. But the team then spent two painful evenings discovering that Ben's codebase had environment-specific build failures, Cursor token limits locked developers out mid-session, and nobody could independently verify which branch of the code actually worked. By Friday, BenVH was deploying to AWS while Azmain filled spreadsheets with synthetic data, and the team was using personal AI subscriptions (Claude at 18 pounds/month, Gemini free tier) because corporate Cursor allocations had been exhausted.
