## Executive Summary

Week 2 was defined by the transition from building to showing. The team spent the first half fighting deployment infrastructure -- schema drift between local and production databases caused cascading failures, duplicate GitHub Actions workflows created confusing build results, and the application load balancer routing had to be fixed by BenVH. The second half delivered the programme's first real validation: an advisory team demo on Wednesday that drew strong positive feedback, and a critical Josh Ellingson feedback session on Thursday that shaped CLARA's adoption strategy.

The advisory demo on January 15 was the week's watershed. Running from Azmain's localhost (production was still broken), the team showed CLARA to Liz, Stacy, Christine, and Steve Gentilli. The response was remarkably positive. Liz declared it clean and intuitive, giving the strongest endorsement the team had received. More importantly, the session produced high-quality product feedback: Stacy insisted on mandatory action plans for every blocker, Christine requested stagnant-account detection via last-modified tracking, Steve raised multi-select archetypes, and Liz proposed a favourites/watch list feature for non-CSM stakeholders. Ben Brooks held the v1 scope boundary firmly -- migration-focused only, no general account management, free-form blockers with guidance rather than rigid categories.

Josh Ellingson's Thursday session proved equally pivotal. Josh's overriding concern was data interpretation reaching leadership before CSMs could validate it -- he specifically warned about Andy Frappe locking onto incorrect data points. Ben Brooks's mid-call arrival produced a critical reframing: the messaging should be about data visibility, not data quality. This single phrase changed the entire conversation's tone and gave Josh a framework he could use with his CSMs. Josh proposed a champion model (2-3 CSMs per region as feedback collators), warned about champion fatigue across parallel programmes, and raised the unresolved question of where CLARA ends and Gainsight begins. His closing assessment was telling: all his pushback was about data quality, not function.

Behind the scenes, the Sales Recon executive meeting preparation intensified. Richard and Azmain aligned with Jamie Stark's team on a five-section agenda for January 26, discovered Idris (banking) was 4-5 months ahead with a copilot studio agent, and identified a critical Gainsight API blocker -- no programmatic access until an internal security review completes (possibly March). Colin Holmes formally established AI training as a workstream (WS6) and the team agreed on a solution-focused training approach rather than tool-centric instruction.
