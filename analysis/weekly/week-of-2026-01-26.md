## Executive Summary

Week 4 was the programme's inflection point. Two events on January 26 changed its trajectory: the Sales Recon executive session in the morning and the portfolio review kickoff with CSMs in the afternoon. Together, they moved CLARA from a prototype being debugged by three people to a tool with executive endorsement, a cross-divisional integration roadmap, and a live user base.

The Sales Recon executive session brought together -- for the first time collectively -- Insurance CS, Banking CS, Corporate CS, the Life team, and the Applied AI/Sales Recon team. Ari LaHavi articulated the core vision: Sales Recon as a guided intelligence layer that knows what it needs to close deals, surfaces gaps, and nudges users for missing information. Jamie presented the platform's year of development work and offered to make the Salesforce connector available to internal agent builders -- a significant concession that would eliminate duplicate data extraction work across teams. Ben Brooks framed CLARA as deliberately bespoke to insurance's IRP migration problem, not a generic CRM competitor, and the consensus tilted toward bidirectional API integration rather than platform consolidation. The strategic question from Week 3 -- absorption or independence? -- was answered with a third option: coexistence with defined interfaces. Craig from Corporate CS raised an important gap around CSM-driven lead identification (CSQL), and Idris confirmed banking's quantitative scoring model. Diya's influence was visible behind the scenes: she had expanded the meeting's invite list, signalling executive sponsorship for cross-team coordination.

Hours later, CLARA went live. The portfolio review kickoff introduced the broader CSM team to the tool for the first time. Natalia ran the session, Azmain demoed, and Ben Brooks provided the strategic narrative: twelve months of building capabilities in isolation (onboarding, adoption charters, solution blueprints, hiring), now brought together with operational visibility. Diya spoke to position CLARA as critical to the insurance scorecard and Andy Frappe's visibility requirements. Josh Ellingson's resistance resurfaced -- he questioned whether CSMs should use the tracker or Salesforce -- prompting visible frustration from Ben Brooks. The data timeline was locked: Wednesday Salesforce cutoff, Thursday build and release, Friday for implementation leads to populate data, Monday February 2 as the first live portfolio review.

The day after, sprint planning confirmed the development priorities: data quality first, RBAC second, audit trail third, weekly update feature fourth. Azmain and Martin split the work. Ben Brooks identified Watson as the bellwether user -- if Watson adopts, everyone follows. A partner workflow workshop in the afternoon revealed a new stakeholder group with legitimate but deferrable needs: partner assignments, partner-specific blockers, and regional tracking. Azmain committed to starting partner features the week after CSM launch stabilised. The programme had transitioned from "can we build it?" to "can we operationalise it?"
