## Executive Summary

Week 8 was the programme's most consequential governance week. The long-awaited Diya session on February 23 produced the single most important strategic outcome since the programme began: Diya endorsed the three-pillar programme structure (IRP portfolio governance, platform embedded intelligence, internal productivity and revenue acceleration), directed CLARA to stay focused on insurance IRP with no Life roadmap, and identified Sales Recon as the only cross-OU integration requiring alignment. The twelve-week resource plan was confirmed: Martin at 75%, Chris at 50%, Nikhil at 50%, BenVH at 50%, with a rotating grad from April 7. Beyond twelve weeks, the team would need a business case for a dedicated team of three to five people.

The governance session also revealed a dramatic shift in programme politics. The Maps team -- which had previously refused to engage, telling the team it was "not important enough" -- was now offering to help after seeing the programme's demonstrated value. Diya's backing had changed the power dynamics. Ben Brookes was simultaneously shopping CLARA around to Life's leadership team, aiming to have Life's MD endorse the programme to Diya at the Wednesday follow-up. The evangelism strategy was high-risk but aligned with the programme's need to build institutional demand for continued investment.

The rest of the week was consumed by three streams of activity: onboarding new team members, technical infrastructure decisions, and Build in Five scoping. Martin returned from his two-week holiday to discover the scope had expanded significantly during his absence. Three demo scenarios were defined for the March 21 exceedance event: data integration service, custom analytics views, and a webhook notification integration based on a real lost deal with Golden Bear. The CLARA flat-pack concept was articulated: take the CLARA architecture and turn it into a templatised deployment so other organisational units could spin up their own instance, using independent deployments rather than multi-tenant to avoid Salesforce team political pushback.

The AI infrastructure session on February 26 brought Nikhil and Chris formally into the programme. Cat Accelerate's tech debt was surfaced: no separate non-production AWS account, fragile CDK deployment, no unit or integration tests (a four-year problem), and step functions created manually with no backup. Rahul proposed a structured prioritisation framework for tech debt. The Sales Recon pilot delivered disappointing results -- missing parent-child hierarchy, wrong date queries, outdated SSO status -- leading the team to begin building their own interim data pipeline.

Throughout the week, the token budget crisis continued unabated. Azmain burned $750 of Cursor credits in three days. His Claude Max subscription expired. The team scrambled between Windsurf via Moody's SSO, OpenAI key fallback, and Bedrock API access as alternatives.
